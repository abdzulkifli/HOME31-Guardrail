1. Profile & Strategic Alignment

Create the initiative once. Core information will automatically carry into later steps, reducing repeated data entry.

Owner: Initiative team
What you are doingCreating one authoritative initiative record that will carry forward through the full HOME31 journey.
What good looks likeA reviewer can understand the need, owner, strategic link and measurable value without opening a separate paper.
Common mistakeStarting with a preferred solution before the problem, intended outcome and success measure are clear.

Initiative profile

What to enter: A short, unique name describing the change—not only the technology or vendor. Why: The same name follows the initiative through risks, evidence, decisions and portfolio reporting.
Who this is: The senior person who champions the initiative, removes enterprise barriers and supports major decisions.
Who this is: The role ultimately answerable for the business outcome, controls and benefits. Use one accountable owner.
Who this is: The person coordinating day-to-day planning, delivery, issues and reporting. This may differ from the accountable owner.
What to select: The decision point the initiative is currently preparing to pass. Select the present gate—not the final target gate.
Good entry: State the present problem, affected users, evidence of the need, and why action is required now. Avoid describing the proposed solution only.

HOME31 Golden Thread

What to select: The single HOME31 pillar most directly accountable for the initiative’s principal outcome. Other pillars may be supporting alignments.
What to enter: The organisation-level condition that should improve, such as financial resilience, customer trust, digital maturity or workforce capability.
Required format: Current baseline → measurable target → achievement date. Example: Reduce verification time from 2 working days to 4 hours by December 2027.
Golden Thread test: A reader should understand the strategic purpose, intended outcome and measurable value without opening the full business case.

2. Initiative Readiness Check

Assess whether the initiative has sufficient strategic, governance, value and control evidence to move through its current gate.

Decision support, not automatic approval
How to rate: Consider the highest credible residual exposure across strategy, operations, finance, policy, people, technology and reputation. High or extreme risk requires treatment and escalation.
What to record: Important assumptions, known limitations, reviewer observations or reasons why an item is not applicable. Keep it concise and decision-relevant.
How to answer the checklist: Tick an item only when supporting evidence exists or an authorised reviewer has confirmed it. A planned action alone is not the same as completed evidence.

70–84%Proceed only with clearly assigned conditions.
55–69%Rework gaps and resubmit for review.

3. Supporting Controls

Record only what is essential for traceability: key risks, decision evidence, measurable benefits and accountability.

Minimum viable governance record
Purpose: Record the few risks that could materially affect the initiative or its gate decision. Do not copy the entire departmental risk register.

Add key risk

Write as cause → event → impact: Because access controls are weak, unauthorised users may view applicant data, causing privacy, regulatory and trust impacts.
Meaning: The seriousness of the consequence if the risk occurs, considering service, finance, compliance, people and reputation.
Meaning: The reasonable probability of occurrence within the initiative period, based on evidence and current controls.
Who to name: The role with authority and resources to manage the exposure—not merely the person recording it.
What date: The deadline for completing the treatment or escalating the unresolved risk before the relevant gate.
Good treatment: A specific action, expected control effect, responsible party, evidence of completion and escalation route.
Purpose: Create an evidence index showing what supports the decision, its status, owner and retrievable location. This is not intended to replace the official document repository.

Add decision evidence

What to select: The category that best explains how the document supports the current gate decision.
Status meaning: Available = approved/current; Draft = still under review; Missing = required but absent; Not Applicable = justified as irrelevant.
Who to name: The role responsible for the accuracy, approval and availability of the evidence.
What to enter: A retrievable folder path, document ID, approved URL, paper reference or clear note explaining where it can be obtained.
Purpose: Define the measurable value expected from the initiative. An output such as “system implemented” is not a benefit unless it produces a measurable improvement.

Add benefit and KPI

Good entry: Describe the improved condition, such as faster turnaround, lower cost, fewer errors, higher trust or stronger capability.
Meaning: The verified current performance before implementation. Without a baseline, improvement cannot be demonstrated.
What to enter: A numeric or objectively testable target with a deadline, tolerance or expected range.
Who to name: The business role accountable for measuring, explaining and sustaining the realised benefit after go-live.
How to choose: Match the frequency to how quickly performance changes and how often management can take corrective action.
What to enter: The authoritative system, report, survey or calculation that will produce the KPI result consistently.
Status meaning: Planned = not yet measured; On track = trajectory supports target; At risk = intervention required; Realised = target evidenced and accepted.
Purpose: Clarify who performs, owns, advises and receives updates for a material activity or decision. Avoid naming too many people as Accountable.

Add accountability record

What to enter: One specific action, deliverable or decision that needs clear ownership.
R: Performs or coordinates the work. More than one role may be responsible where necessary.
A: Owns the final outcome and approval. Prefer one accountable role for each activity.
C: Provides specialist input before the work or decision is finalised; communication is two-way.
I: Receives status or the final outcome but is not required to approve it; communication is generally one-way.
Minimum expectation before decision: At least one measurable benefit, relevant evidence records, and treatment for any high or extreme risk.

4. Stage-Gate Decision

Bring the assessment, risks, evidence and benefits together into a traceable management decision.

Owner: Authorised decision forum

Decision record

What to select: The exact G0–G8 decision point being considered by the authorised forum.
What this means: The authorised outcome after considering readiness, risk, evidence, affordability, dependencies and strategic priority.
Who to enter: The committee, sponsor or delegated authority formally empowered to make this gate decision.
What date: The date the authorised decision was made, not the date the form was prepared.
When required: Use for conditional approvals or corrective actions. Leave blank only when genuinely not applicable.
Good decision record: State each condition or reason, responsible owner, deadline, evidence required for closure and escalation route if overdue.

G0–G8 lifecycle

Six non-negotiable rules

1. Strategic fit

No initiative proceeds without a visible HOME31 alignment and enterprise outcome.

2. Named ownership

Sponsor, accountable owner, delivery lead and benefits owner must be clear.

3. Decision evidence

Material assumptions, risks, costs, approvals and conditions must be traceable.

4. Risk appetite

Material exposure must be treated or escalated before funding and go-live.

5. Benefits discipline

Benefits require a baseline, target, owner, source and review date.

6. Formal closure

Closure includes benefits review, handover, residual actions and lessons learned.

HOME31 Guardrail Maturity Assessment

This is a periodic organisation-level assessment—not a mandatory step for every initiative. It evaluates whether the guardrail is embedded as a living execution discipline.

Suggested owner: CPS + governance functions
Recommended use: Complete annually, or after a major implementation phase. Use the result to prioritise enterprise improvement actions.
